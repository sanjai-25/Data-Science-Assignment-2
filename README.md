Data-Driven Demand Forecasting and Price Sensitivity AnalysisThis project explores the relationship between market variables and consumer demand using a dataset of 35,000 records. By leveraging Python libraries such as Pandas for data manipulation and Seaborn/Matplotlib for visualization, the study performs a comprehensive Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) to identify key drivers of sales. The analysis specifically focuses on the correlation between Price and Demand, while accounting for external factors including Marketing Campaigns, Seasonal Trends, and Competitor Pricing.Initial statistical summaries reveal a weak negative correlation (approx -0.04) between price and demand within the observed range, suggesting that other features—such as seasonal effects (e.g., Summer/Winter multipliers) and stock availability—may play a more dominant role in driving volume. The workflow includes rigorous data cleaning (handling missing values in marketing data and removing duplicates) to ensure model integrity. This research provides a foundational framework for developing predictive machine learning models aimed at optimizing inventory levels and pricing strategies in a competitive retail environment.
